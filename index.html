bllr Milestones

2016 Idea Formation

I came up with bllr while reflecting on social contract theory during a graduate philosophy course. Social contract theory emphasizes that in order for members of a society to live together peacefully, we must at least maintain a high level of integrity. Reflecting on this theory, I began to think of the last time I'd been dishonest. I then realized that it was during a pick-up basketball game. During the game, I had lost track of how many points I'd scored and when asked, I said I scored more than what was true, leading to a heated argument. Reflecting, I realized this situation occurred virtually every time I played pick-up basketball; either myself or another player would eventually lose track of the game score and how many points they contributed to it.

Since there is no visual scoreboard in pick-up basketball, there is no way to easily remember the score, thus leading to frequent misreportingâ€”It was then that I realized pick-up basketball lacked a robust social contract, but could easily adapt one with a real-time wearable scoring system.

2021 Fully-functional Prototype Developed

bllr version 1.1 was developed using Jetpack Compose and Kotlin, running on the android wear development system. We chose to develop with android so that we could emulate having our own wristband, rather than using the Apple Watch. Ultimately, we want basketball players to have their own unique wearable, not just another app on the Apple Watch.

We can't wait to develop bllr version 2.0 with an entirely modern look and updated functionality that can track both health and basketball data-metrics!
